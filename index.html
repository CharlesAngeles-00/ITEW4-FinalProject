About This Project

Educational Resource on Philippine Governance

This comprehensive digital platform is dedicated to documenting and analyzing the leadership of all Philippine presidents throughout history. Our mission is to provide accurate, engaging, and accessible information about the individuals who have shaped the nation's political landscape.

The project was developed by Websponsive, a team of IT students passionate about creating educational resources that promote civic awareness and historical understanding.

Presidential Timeline

First Republic (1899-1901)

Emilio Aguinaldo becomes the first president of the Philippines during the revolutionary government.

Commonwealth Era (1935-1946)

Manuel L. Quezon serves as the first president of the Commonwealth government under American rule.

Post-War Period (1946-1965)

Philippines gains full independence. Presidents Manuel Roxas, Elpidio Quirino, Ramon Magsaysay, Carlos P. Garcia, and Diosdado Macapagal serve during this period.

Marcos Era (1965-1986)

Ferdinand Marcos rules for 20 years, including 14 years under martial law, until ousted by the People Power Revolution.

Restoration of Democracy (1986-1998)

Corazon Aquino leads the country after Marcos, followed by Fidel V. Ramos who implements economic reforms.

Modern Era (1998-Present)

Joseph Estrada, Gloria Macapagal-Arroyo, Benigno Aquino III, Rodrigo Duterte, and the current president lead the country through various challenges.
