Journey Through Philippine Leadership

Explore the history, achievements, and governance styles of all Philippine presidents from the First Republic to the present.

Explore Presidents

Philippine Presidents

Emilio Aguinaldo

1899-1901

First President of the Philippines. Led the Philippine Revolution against Spain and the Philippine-American War.

View Details

Manuel L. Quezon

1935-1944

First president of the Commonwealth of the Philippines. Known as the "Father of the National Language" for promoting Tagalog as the national language.

View Details

Jose P. Laurel

1943-1945

President of the Japanese-sponsored Republic during World War II. Later recognized as having taken the position to protect Filipinos during occupation.

View Details

Sergio Osmeña

1944-1946

Second president of the Commonwealth. Helped restore democratic institutions after Japanese occupation during World War II.

View Details

Manuel Roxas

1946-1948

First President of the independent Republic of the Philippines. Handled post-war reconstruction and established economic ties with the United States.

View Details

Elpidio Quirino

1948-1953

Continued post-war reconstruction efforts and faced challenges with the Huk rebellion. Known for his reconciliation policy with Japan.

View Details

Ramon Magsaysay

1953-1957

Known as the "Champion of the Masses." His administration was noted for its integrity and reforms to help the rural poor.

View Details

Carlos P. Garcia

1957-1961

Known for his "Filipino First" policy that prioritized Filipino businesses. Promoted nationalism and cultural identity.

View Details

Diosdado Macapagal

1961-1965

Known as the "Poor boy from Lubao." Changed Independence Day from July 4 to June 12 and implemented land reform programs.

View Details

Ferdinand Marcos

1965-1986

Longest-serving president who declared martial law in 1972. His regime was marked by authoritarian rule and corruption.

View Details

Corazon Aquino

1986-1992

First female president of the Philippines and Asia. She restored democratic institutions after the Marcos dictatorship.

View Details

Fidel V. Ramos

1992-1998

Known for economic reforms and political stability. His administration saw significant economic growth.

View Details

Joseph Estrada

1998-2001

Former actor who was elected with wide popular support. His term was cut short due to impeachment proceedings and the EDSA II revolution.

View Details

Gloria Macapagal Arroyo

2001-2010

Economist who focused on fiscal reforms. Second female president and daughter of former President Diosdado Macapagal.

View Details

Benigno Aquino III

2010-2016

Son of former President Corazon Aquino. His administration focused on anti-corruption measures and economic growth.

View Details

Rodrigo Duterte

2016-2022

Known for his controversial war on drugs and populist approach. Former mayor of Davao City who focused on crime reduction.

View Details

Ferdinand Marcos Jr.

2022-Present

Son of former President Ferdinand Marcos. Current administration focusing on economic recovery and national unity.

View Details
