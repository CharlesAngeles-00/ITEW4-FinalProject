Diosdado Macapagal

9th President of the Philippines (1961-1965)

"The Poor Boy from Lubao" and "Champion of Land Reform"

Biography

Diosdado Pangan Macapagal served as the ninth president of the Philippines, known for his integrity programs and economic reforms. A lawyer and economist, he rose from poverty to become president, earning him the nickname "The Poor Boy from Lubao."

Early Life

Born on September 28, 1910 in Lubao, Pampanga to a poor farming family. His parents were Urbano Macapagal and Romana Pangan. Despite poverty, he excelled academically, earning degrees from University of the Philippines and University of Santo Tomas.

Presidency

Macapagal was elected president in 1961, defeating Carlos P. Garcia. His administration focused on fighting corruption, land reform, and shifting Independence Day from July 4 to June 12.

Key Achievements

  • Changed Independence Day to June 12
  • Implemented Agricultural Land Reform Code
  • Established the first Philippine Stock Exchange

Quick Facts

  • Born: September 28, 1910
  • Died: April 21, 1997
  • Political Party: Liberal Party
  • Notable Family: Father of President Gloria Macapagal-Arroyo

Legacy

Macapagal's presidency is remembered for its emphasis on moral governance and economic nationalism. His most enduring legacy was changing the celebration of Philippine Independence from July 4 to June 12, correcting the historical record to honor Aguinaldo's 1898 declaration.

The Agricultural Land Reform Code, though limited in implementation, became the foundation for future agrarian reform efforts. Macapagal also initiated the Maphilindo concept, an early attempt at regional cooperation with Malaysia and Indonesia. His daughter Gloria would later become the 14th Philippine president, making them the first father-daughter presidential combination.
