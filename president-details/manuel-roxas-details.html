Manuel Roxas

5th President of the Philippines (1946-1948)

"The Last President of the Commonwealth and First of the Third Republic"

Biography

Manuel Acuña Roxas served as the fifth president of the Philippines and the first president of the independent Third Philippine Republic. His presidency marked the transition from U.S. Commonwealth to full sovereignty, though his term was cut short by his untimely death.

Early Life

Born on January 1, 1892 in Capiz (now Roxas City) to a prominent political family. His parents were Gerardo Roxas and Rosario Acuña. He graduated as valedictorian from Manila High School and top of his class at the University of the Philippines College of Law.

Presidency

Roxas was elected in April 1946 and inaugurated on July 4, 1946, the day the Philippines gained full independence from the United States. His administration focused on postwar reconstruction and establishing the new republic's institutions.

Key Achievements

  • Oversaw formal independence on July 4, 1946
  • Established the Central Bank of the Philippines
  • Negotiated the Bell Trade Act and War Damage claims

Quick Facts

  • Born: January 1, 1892
  • Died: April 15, 1948 (age 56)
  • Political Party: Liberal Party (co-founder)
  • Predecessor: Sergio Osmeña

Legacy

Roxas's brief presidency established important foundations for the new republic, though his acceptance of certain U.S. conditions for independence remains controversial. His death from a heart attack at Clark Air Base made him the second Philippine president to die in office.

The Roxas political dynasty continues through descendants like his grandson, former Senator Mar Roxas. His hometown was renamed Roxas City in his honor, and his image appears on the 100-peso bill, commemorating his role in Philippine independence.
