Fidel V. Ramos

1992-1998

Biography

Fidel Valdez Ramos served as the 12th president of the Philippines from 1992 to 1998. A career military officer, he played a crucial role in the 1986 EDSA Revolution and became known for his economic reforms and infrastructure development programs.

Early Life

Born on March 18, 1928 in Lingayen, Pangasinan. His parents were Narciso Ramos, a lawyer and congressman, and Angela Valdez, an educator.

Presidency

Ramos won the 1992 elections with just 23.6% of the vote in a crowded field. His administration focused on national reconciliation, economic recovery, and infrastructure development.

Legacy

Ramos is remembered for stabilizing the Philippine economy after years of crisis, achieving an average GDP growth rate of 3.9% during his term. His "Philippines 2000" program aimed to make the country newly industrialized by the year 2000.

As the first Protestant president of the predominantly Catholic Philippines, Ramos championed religious tolerance. His administration saw the resolution of the 24-year Moro rebellion in Mindanao through the 1996 Peace Agreement with Nur Misuari's MNLF.

Key Achievements

  • Philippines 2000 development framework
  • Signing of the Final Peace Agreement with MNLF
  • Liberalization of key industries

Quick Facts

  • Born: March 18, 1928
  • Died: July 31, 2022
  • Political Party: Lakas-NUCD
  • Military Rank: General (AFP)
