Benigno Aquino III

15th President of the Philippines (2010-2016)

"Daang Matuwid" (The Straight Path)

Biography

Benigno Simeon "Noynoy" Cojuangco Aquino III served as the 15th president of the Philippines from 2010 to 2016. The only son of democracy icons Ninoy and Cory Aquino, he rode a wave of public sympathy following his mother's death to win the presidency on an anti-corruption platform.

Early Life

Born on February 8, 1960 in Manila. His parents were Senator Benigno "Ninoy" Aquino Jr. and President Corazon Aquino. He graduated with an Economics degree from Ateneo de Manila University and worked in various business positions before entering politics.

Presidency

Elected in 2010 with the highest margin in Philippine electoral history at that time (15 million votes), Aquino's administration focused on good governance, anti-corruption, and economic reforms under his "Daang Matuwid" (Straight Path) slogan.

Key Achievements

  • Passed the Responsible Parenthood and Reproductive Health Law
  • Oversaw economic growth averaging 6.2% annually
  • Initiated the successful arbitration case against China's maritime claims

Quick Facts

  • Born: February 8, 1960
  • Died: June 24, 2021
  • Political Party: Liberal Party
  • Notable: First bachelor president

Legacy

Aquino's administration restored international confidence in the Philippine economy, achieving investment-grade credit ratings for the first time in history. His anti-corruption campaign led to the impeachment of Chief Justice Renato Corona and investigations into previous administration's anomalies.

The 2016 arbitral ruling that invalidated China's expansive South China Sea claims stands as a landmark foreign policy achievement, though enforcement remained challenging. Aquino's term ended with high satisfaction ratings, though critics noted unfinished reforms in infrastructure and poverty reduction.
