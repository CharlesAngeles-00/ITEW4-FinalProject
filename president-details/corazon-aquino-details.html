Corazon Aquino

11th President of the Philippines (1986-1992)

"Mother of Philippine Democracy"

Biography

Maria Corazon "Cory" Cojuangco Aquino served as the 11th president of the Philippines and the first female president in Asia. She came to power after the 1986 People Power Revolution that toppled Ferdinand Marcos' dictatorship.

Early Life

Born on January 25, 1933 in Paniqui, Tarlac to a wealthy political family. She married Benigno "Ninoy" Aquino Jr., Marcos' chief political rival, in 1954.

Presidency

Assumed the presidency on February 25, 1986 after Marcos fled the country. Her administration focused on restoring democratic institutions and writing a new constitution.

Key Achievements

  • Restoration of democratic government
  • 1987 Constitution ratification
  • Comprehensive Agrarian Reform Program

Quick Facts

  • Born: January 25, 1933
  • Died: August 1, 2009
  • Political Party: UNIDO/PDP-Laban

Legacy

Aquino is celebrated as the icon of Philippine democracy who led the country's transition from authoritarian rule. Her presidency established democratic institutions that remain in place today, including the 1987 Constitution that limited presidential power.

Despite facing numerous coup attempts and natural disasters during her term, Aquino peacefully transferred power to her successor Fidel Ramos in 1992 - the first peaceful transition in decades. She remained active in social and political causes until her death from cancer in 2009.
