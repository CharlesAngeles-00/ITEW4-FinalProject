Carlos P. Garcia

1957-1961

Biography

Carlos Polistico Garcia served as the eighth president of the Philippines, succeeding Ramon Magsaysay after his tragic death. A distinguished poet and politician, Garcia is best remembered for his "Filipino First" economic policy and his contributions to Philippine cultural development.

Born on November 4, 1896 in Talibon, Bohol, his parents were Policronio Garcia, a municipal mayor, and Ambrosia Polistico, a teacher. He earned his law degree from Philippine Law School while working as a teacher and clerk. Garcia assumed the presidency on March 18, 1957 following Magsaysay's plane crash, and was elected to a full term later that year. His administration emphasized nationalist economic policies and cultural revival.

Legacy

Garcia's nationalist economic policies, particularly his "Filipino First" policy, sought to prioritize Filipino businesses over foreign interests. While controversial at the time, these policies laid groundwork for economic nationalism. His administration also made significant strides in cultural development, promoting Filipino arts and literature.

As a respected poet in the Boholano dialect, Garcia's literary contributions earned him posthumous recognition as a National Artist for Literature. The Carlos P. Garcia Foundation continues to promote his vision of cultural nationalism, and his hometown in Bohol has become a historical and tourist site.

Quick Facts

  • Born: November 4, 1896
  • Died: June 14, 1971
  • Political Party: Nacionalista Party
  • Notable: Awarded National Artist for Literature (posthumously)

Key Achievements

  • Implemented the "Filipino First" policy
  • Established the Austerity Program
  • Promoted Filipino cultural arts and language
