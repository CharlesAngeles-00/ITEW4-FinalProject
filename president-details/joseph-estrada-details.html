Joseph Estrada

1998-2001

Biography

Joseph Ejercito Estrada served as the 13th president of the Philippines from 1998 until his ouster in 2001. A former movie star turned politician, he was the first Philippine president to be impeached and removed from office.

Early Life

Born on April 19, 1937 in Tondo, Manila as José Marcelo Ejercito. His parents were Emilio Ejercito and Maria Marcelo. He adopted the screen name "Joseph Estrada" for his acting career where he became famous playing action hero roles.

Presidency

Estrada won the 1998 elections by landslide with his pro-poor platform. His administration focused on programs for the poor but was cut short by corruption allegations and the 2001 EDSA People Power II revolution.

Legacy

Estrada's presidency remains one of the most controversial in modern Philippine history. While immensely popular among the masses for his pro-poor stance and charismatic persona, his administration was plagued by corruption allegations that led to his unprecedented removal from office.

Despite his conviction for plunder in 2007 (later pardoned), Estrada made a political comeback, serving as Mayor of Manila from 2013-2019. His presidency demonstrated both the enduring power of celebrity in Philippine politics and the continuing strength of People Power as a political force.

Key Events

  • Launched anti-poverty programs
  • Faced impeachment for plunder charges
  • Ousted in EDSA People Power II (2001)

Quick Facts

  • Born: April 19, 1937
  • Age at Inauguration: 61 years
  • Political Party: PMP (Partido ng Masang Pilipino)
  • Vice President: Gloria Macapagal-Arroyo
