Rodrigo Duterte

2016-2022

Biography

Rodrigo Roa Duterte served as the 16th president of the Philippines from 2016 to 2022. Known for his controversial war on drugs and blunt speaking style, the former Davao City mayor was the first president from Mindanao.

Early Life

Born on March 28, 1945 in Maasin, Southern Leyte. His parents were Vicente Duterte, a provincial governor, and Soledad Roa, a teacher and activist. He earned his political science degree from Lyceum of the Philippines and law degree from San Beda College.

Presidency

Duterte won the 2016 elections by landslide with his tough-on-crime platform. His administration focused on anti-drug campaigns, infrastructure development, and an independent foreign policy shifting toward China and Russia.

Legacy

Duterte's presidency was marked by unprecedented controversy and polarization. While his drug war earned international condemnation and ICC investigation, it remained popular domestically. His infrastructure program saw record investments but faced completion challenges.

The administration's foreign policy shift to China proved contentious as maritime disputes continued. Duterte maintained high approval ratings throughout his term despite criticism, reflecting his strong connection with his base. His daughter Sara Duterte's election as vice president in 2022 signaled the family's continuing political influence.

Key Policies

  • Controversial war on drugs with thousands killed
  • Build Build Build infrastructure program
  • Federalism initiative (unrealized)
  • Independent foreign policy pivot

Quick Facts

  • Born: March 28, 1945
  • Political Party: PDP-Laban
  • Previous Position: Davao City Mayor (22 years)
  • Vice President: Leni Robredo (opposition)
