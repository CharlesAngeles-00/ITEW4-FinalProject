Gloria Macapagal Arroyo

14th President of the Philippines (2001-2010)

"The Economist President"

Biography

Gloria Macapagal Arroyo served as the 14th president of the Philippines, initially succeeding Joseph Estrada after EDSA People Power II in 2001, then winning her own term in 2004. An economist by training, she was the country's second female president.

Early Life

Born on April 5, 1947 in San Juan, Manila to President Diosdado Macapagal and Evangelina Macaraeg. She earned economics degrees from Georgetown University (where she was classmates with Bill Clinton) and the University of the Philippines.

Presidency

Arroyo assumed the presidency on January 20, 2001 and was elected to a full term in 2004. Her administration focused on economic reforms but faced numerous political crises and corruption allegations.

Key Policies

  • Implemented Expanded Value Added Tax (EVAT) reform
  • Oversaw strong economic growth (7% GDP peak)
  • Launched infrastructure programs like the Super Region strategy

Quick Facts

  • Born: April 5, 1947
  • Political Party: Lakas-Kampi-CMD
  • Education: Ph.D. in Economics
  • Post-Presidency: Served as Pampanga Congresswoman and House Speaker

Legacy

Arroyo's presidency delivered significant economic growth and fiscal reforms, but was overshadowed by political controversies including the "Hello Garci" election scandal and multiple impeachment attempts. Her technocratic approach stabilized government finances but struggled with perceptions of corruption.

After her presidency, Arroyo was detained on plunder charges but was later acquitted. She made a remarkable political comeback, serving in Congress and eventually becoming the first woman Speaker of the House of Representatives. Her complex legacy combines economic achievements with persistent questions about governance.
