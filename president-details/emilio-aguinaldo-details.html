Emilio Aguinaldo

1st President of the Philippines (1899-1901)

"Presidente ng Unang Republika"

Biography

Emilio Aguinaldo y Famy was a Filipino revolutionary, statesman, and military leader who is officially recognized as the first president of the Philippines (1899-1901) and led Philippine forces first against Spain in the latter part of the Philippine Revolution (1896-1898), and then in the Philippine-American War (1899-1901).

Early Life

Born on March 22, 1869 in Kawit, Cavite. His parents were Carlos Aguinaldo and Trinidad Famy, a Chinese mestizo couple from a wealthy family.

Presidency

Aguinaldo proclaimed Philippine independence on June 12, 1898 and established the First Philippine Republic. He served as president from January 23, 1899 until his capture by American forces on March 23, 1901.

Key Achievements

  • Proclaimed Philippine Independence on June 12, 1898
  • Established the First Philippine Republic
  • Led revolutionary forces against Spain and America

Quick Facts

  • Born: March 22, 1869
  • Died: February 6, 1964 (aged 94)
  • Political Affiliation: Magdalo faction
  • Notable Work: Memoirs of the Revolution

Legacy

Aguinaldo's legacy remains complex and controversial. While he is officially recognized as the first president and the man who declared Philippine independence, his role in the execution of revolutionary hero Andres Bonifacio and his subsequent collaboration with American colonial authorities have been subjects of historical debate.

In his later years, Aguinaldo became a vocal advocate for veterans' rights and Philippine independence. He lived long enough to see the fulfillment of his independence dream when the Philippines gained full sovereignty in 1946. The Aguinaldo Shrine in Kawit, Cavite, where independence was proclaimed, remains an important historical site.
