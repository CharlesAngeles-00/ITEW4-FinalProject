Emilio Aguinaldo

1899-1901

Biography

Emilio Aguinaldo was born on March 22, 1869, in Cavite, Philippines. He played a pivotal role in the Philippine Revolution against Spanish colonial rule and later led the resistance against American occupation during the Philippine-American War.

Rising to prominence as a young leader, Aguinaldo became the first President of the Philippines, establishing the First Philippine Republic in 1899. His leadership was marked by efforts to assert national independence.

Legacy

Aguinaldo's legacy is complex, celebrated for his role in the fight for independence but debated due to his controversial decisions, including the execution of Andres Bonifacio and his eventual capture by American forces.

His contributions to Philippine sovereignty remain a significant part of the nation's history, with his leadership inspiring future generations of Filipinos.

Quick Facts

  • Born: March 22, 1869, Cavite
  • Died: February 6, 1964
  • Education: Colegio de San Juan de Letran
  • Spouse: Hilaria del Rosario, Maria Agoncillo

Key Achievements

  • Led the Philippine Revolution against Spain
  • Proclaimed Philippine Independence on June 12, 1898
  • Established the First Philippine Republic
  • Organized resistance during the Philippine-American War
