Manuel L. Quezon

1935-1944

Biography

Manuel Luis Quezon y Molina served as the president of the Commonwealth of the Philippines from 1935 to 1944. He was the first Filipino to head a government of the entire Philippines and is considered the second president of the Philippines after Emilio Aguinaldo.

Early Life

Born on August 19, 1878 in Baler, Tayabas (now Aurora). His parents were Lucio Quezon and Maria Dolores Molina.

Presidency

Quezon was elected president in 1935, defeating Emilio Aguinaldo and Gregorio Aglipay in the Philippines' first national presidential election.

Legacy

Quezon is best remembered for his efforts to establish a Filipino national identity through the promotion of a national language. He established the Institute of National Language which selected Tagalog as the basis for the national language in 1937.

His leadership during the Commonwealth period helped prepare the Philippines for eventual independence, though World War II interrupted these plans.

Key Achievements

  • Established Tagalog as the basis for the national language
  • Created the National Council of Education
  • Initiated women's suffrage

Quick Facts

  • Born: August 19, 1878
  • Died: August 1, 1944
  • Political Party: Nacionalista
