Ferdinand Marcos

1965-1986

Biography

Ferdinand Emmanuel Edralin Marcos served as the 10th president of the Philippines from 1965 to 1986. He ruled under martial law from 1972 to 1981, and his authoritarian regime was marked by corruption, human rights abuses, and economic mismanagement.

Born on September 11, 1917 in Sarrat, Ilocos Norte, his parents were Mariano Marcos, a lawyer and politician, and Josefa Edralin, a teacher. Marcos was first elected president in 1965 and re-elected in 1969. He declared martial law in 1972, extending his rule until he was ousted by the People Power Revolution in 1986.

Legacy

Marcos's 21-year rule remains the most controversial in Philippine history. While his administration built numerous infrastructure projects, his regime was marked by widespread human rights violations, media suppression, and the plunder of an estimated $5-10 billion from the Philippine treasury.

The 1986 People Power Revolution that ousted Marcos restored democracy but left lasting political and economic scars. The Marcos family has since attempted political rehabilitation, with family members returning to government positions.

Quick Facts

  • Born: September 11, 1917
  • Died: September 28, 1989
  • Political Party: Kilusang Bagong Lipunan

Key Events

  • Declaration of Martial Law (1972)
  • Massive infrastructure projects
  • Economic collapse in early 1980s
